Module 6: Idiomatic Expressions

A. Objectives

  • Recognize idiomatic expressions in short conversations
  • Understand the meaning of idioms based on context

B. Material Description

Idioms are expressions whose meanings cannot be understood from the literal definitions of the individual words. They are commonly used in everyday spoken English, especially in informal contexts.

  • Hit the books = Study hard
  • Break the ice = Make people feel more comfortable in a new situation
  • Under the weather = Feeling ill

6.a. Study and Academic Idioms

Woman: Are you joining the party tonight?
Man: I wish I could, but I really have to hit the books.
Answer: A. He has to study.

Exercise 6.1

Identify idioms related to study or school, and choose the correct interpretation.

6.b. Social Situations Idioms

Man: I was nervous during the meeting until Sarah told a joke to break the ice.
Answer: C. Sarah helped ease the tension.

Exercise 6.2

Underline idioms related to social interaction and explain their function in the dialogue.

6.c. Health and Feelings Idioms

Woman: You don’t look so good today.
Man: Yeah, I’m feeling a bit under the weather.
Answer: B. He is not feeling well.

Exercise 6.3

Find idioms that describe physical or emotional conditions and explain their implied meaning.

6.d. Everyday Idiomatic Expressions

  • Piece of cake = Something very easy
  • Cost an arm and a leg = Very expensive
  • Hit the sack = Go to sleep
Man: That test was a piece of cake!
Narrator: What does the man mean?
Answer: C. The test was very easy.

Exercise 6.4

Identify idioms used in everyday situations and interpret their real meaning.

C. Review Exercises

Identify idiomatic expressions in short conversations and interpret their meanings correctly.

Review Example:

Woman: I can’t believe you bought that bag!
Man: I know. It cost me an arm and a leg.
Answer: B. The bag was very expensive.
