Modul 3: Negative Expressions

A. Objectives

After studying this material, students are able to:

  • Recognize and understand various types of negatives
  • Recognize and understand the negative expressions found in short conversations

B. Material Description

1.a. Negative Markers

Beberapa ekspresi negatif langsung dapat dikenali dengan adanya kata seperti not, never, atau awalan negatif seperti un-, in-, dis-. Berikut contohnya:

  • Not / n't: e.g., He couldn't pass the test = He failed
  • Never, nobody, none: e.g., Paul never makes his bed = He is lazy
  • Negative prefixes: un-, in-, dis- (e.g., irresponsible = careless)

Example 1:

Woman: Mitch and Lynn must have a lot in common since they’re both economics majors.
Man: Oh, I didn’t realize that Mitch was an economics major too.
Narrator: What does the man imply?

Answer: B. He does not think they own something in common.

Example 2:

Man: Do you think Sue will join us for lunch?
Woman: Not likely. She never eats out during weekdays.
Narrator: What does the woman mean?

Answer: D. Sue probably won’t come.

Exercise 3.1

Listen to the following short conversations. Write down the conversations, underline the negative words, and choose the best answer.

1.b. Double Negatives

Menggabungkan dua kata negatif biasanya menghasilkan arti positif dalam bahasa Inggris. Hati-hati jangan sampai salah mengartikannya secara harfiah.

  • "It is not impossible" = It is possible
  • "Nobody isn't at home" = Everybody is at home

Example 1:

Man: I’d like to return this sweater because it’s too small. I don’t have the receipt though.
Woman: You could exchange the sweater for another size. But if you don’t have the receipt, I won’t be able to give your money back.

Answer: A. He can only get a refund with a receipt.

Example 2:

Woman: Doesn’t nobody care about the results?
Man: Apparently, everyone already knows the outcome.
Narrator: What does the woman imply?

Answer: C. She thinks no one cares.

Exercise 3.2

Underline double negative expressions and choose the best answer.

1.c. Almost Negatives

Kata seperti hardly, rarely, barely, seldom secara makna sangat dekat dengan kata negatif. Mereka menyiratkan bahwa sesuatu jarang atau hampir tidak terjadi.

Example 1:

Man: Only rarely have I tasted such delicious meat.
Narrator: What does the man mean?

Answer: A. This is the best meat he’s tasted in a long time.

Example 2:

Woman: I hardly ever get to see my parents these days.
Narrator: What does the woman mean?

Answer: C. She rarely sees her parents.

Exercise 3.3

Identify and underline almost negative expressions in the dialogues.

1.d. Comparatives with Negatives

Perbandingan negatif seperti “nothing is more...” atau “no one is better than...” biasanya menyiratkan bentuk superlatif (paling...).

Example 1:

Man: Nothing could have been more difficult than that exam.
Narrator: What does the man mean?

Answer: C. The test wasn’t easy at all.

Example 2:

Woman: No one is more dedicated to this project than Lisa.
Narrator: What does the woman imply?

Answer: A. Lisa is the most committed member.

Exercise 3.4

Identify comparative structures with negative markers and select the most accurate meaning.

C. Review Exercises

This is a review of negative forms including simple negatives, double negatives, almost negatives, and negative-comparatives. Analyze the dialogues and determine the intended meaning of each speaker.

Review Example:

Man: Are you going to the wedding?
Woman: I can’t say I’m not interested.
Narrator: What does the woman mean?
Answer: B. She is interested in going.

D. Summary of Negative Expressions

Type Example Implied Meaning
Basic Negative I don't like coffee He dislikes coffee
Negative Prefix Unfriendly, incapable Negative traits/characteristics
Double Negative Not unusual, not impossible Positive meaning
Almost Negative Rarely, hardly, barely Hampir tidak pernah
Comparative Negative No one is better than...
Nothing is more...		 Menyatakan superlatif (paling...)
