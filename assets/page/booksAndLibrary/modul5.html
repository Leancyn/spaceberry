Module 5: Related Time Expressions

A. Objectives

  • Identify expressions related to time
  • Understand temporal relationships using before, after, during, since, etc.

B. Material Description

This module focuses on time-related expressions that help understand the sequence of events in conversations. Common expressions include:

  • Before the meeting
  • After the break
  • During the lecture
  • Since yesterday
  • By the time..., As soon as..., While, Until, When, Recently, Lately

These expressions help define when an event happens and are often used to answer questions accurately in listening exercises.

5.a. Before and After

These prepositions show that one event happens earlier or later than another.

Woman: Will you be free after class?
Man: Yes, we can meet after the lecture ends.
Answer: C. He will be available following the lecture.

Exercise 5.1

Underline the time expressions using "before" or "after" and choose the correct sequence of events.

5.b. During and While

These are used to express events that occur at the same time as another.

Man: I fell asleep during the movie.
Narrator: What does the man mean?
Answer: A. He was sleeping while the movie was playing.

Exercise 5.2

Find overlapping actions with "during" or "while" and describe what happened at the same time.

5.c. Since and For

Since refers to the starting point of time, while for refers to the duration.

Woman: How long have you lived here?
Man: Since 2020.
Answer: C. He has been living here from 2020 until now.

Exercise 5.3

Identify whether the time expression indicates a starting point (since) or a duration (for).

5.d. Other Time Markers

These markers define deadlines or immediate events:

  • Until: up to a time (I waited until 10 PM)
  • By: no later than a time (Finish by Friday)
  • As soon as: immediately after something happens
Man: I’ll submit the paper by tomorrow.
Narrator: What does the man mean?
Answer: B. He will turn in the paper no later than tomorrow.

Exercise 5.4

Match each sentence with the appropriate time expression and its meaning.

C. Review Exercises

Identify the correct sequence of events and match time expressions to the appropriate context in conversations.

Review Example:

Woman: We’ve been working here since last year.
Narrator: What does the woman imply?
Answer: A. They started working last year and are still working.

D. Summary Table

Time Expression Function Example
Before / After Sequence After lunch, we’ll meet.
During / While Simultaneous Events She called during the lecture.
Since / For Duration or starting point He has lived here since 2020.
Until / By / As soon as Deadlines or immediacy I’ll send the email by 5 PM.
