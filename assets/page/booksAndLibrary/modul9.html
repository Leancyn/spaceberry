Module 9: Review Exercises - Idiomatic Expressions

A. Objectives

  • Recognize idiomatic expressions in various short conversations
  • Understand the meaning of idioms based on context
  • Use idiomatic expressions appropriately in speaking and writing
  • Distinguish literal and figurative meanings in idioms

B. Material Description

Idiomatic expressions are phrases where the overall meaning is different from the literal meanings of individual words. They often reflect cultural or contextual meanings and are widely used in everyday English conversations.

Common Idioms Covered:

  • Hit the books – to study hard
  • Break the ice – to start a conversation, especially to relieve tension
  • Under the weather – feeling sick or unwell
  • Piece of cake – something very easy
  • Costs an arm and a leg – very expensive
  • Let the cat out of the bag – to reveal a secret accidentally
  • Once in a blue moon – something that happens very rarely

Understanding idioms enhances comprehension of informal speech, movies, songs, and conversations with native speakers.

Tips for Learning Idioms:

  • Pay attention to the context in which idioms are used.
  • Practice using idioms in your own sentences to remember their meanings.
  • Listen to native speakers in movies or podcasts to hear idioms in action.
  • Don’t translate idioms literally; focus on the overall meaning.

C. Review Exercises

Instructions: Read the conversations below. Identify the idiomatic expression(s) and choose or write the correct meaning. Then, try to use the idioms in your own sentences.

Exercise 9.1: Multiple Choice

Man: I can’t come out tonight. I have to hit the books for my final exams.
Woman: Good luck! I know you’ll do great.
Question: What does “hit the books” mean?
A. To physically hit books
B. To study hard
C. To buy books

Exercise 9.2: Multiple Choice

Woman: The party was so awkward at first.
Man: Yeah, I tried to break the ice with a joke.
Question: What does “break the ice” mean?
A. To start a conversation
B. To smash frozen water
C. To break something fragile

Exercise 9.3: Multiple Choice

Man: Are you feeling okay? You look under the weather.
Woman: I’m just a little sick.
Question: What does “under the weather” mean?
A. Feeling sick
B. Standing outside
C. Feeling happy

Exercise 9.4: Matching

Match the idioms on the left with their meanings on the right.

1. Piece of cake A. Very expensive
2. Costs an arm and a leg B. Something very easy
3. Let the cat out of the bag C. To reveal a secret accidentally
4. Once in a blue moon D. Something that happens rarely

Exercise 9.5: Fill in the blanks

Complete the sentences with the correct idiomatic expressions from the list: hit the books, break the ice, under the weather, piece of cake, costs an arm and a leg, let the cat out of the bag, once in a blue moon.

  1. I don’t want to _________ during the meeting, so I prepared a funny story.
  2. This exam is a ________; I finished it in 10 minutes.
  3. She usually feels ________ when the weather changes.
  4. The new phone ________, so I decided not to buy it.
  5. I accidentally _________ about the surprise party.
  6. We only get to see each other _________ because we live in different countries.
  7. It’s time to _________ if you want to pass the test next week.

Exercise 9.6: Creative Writing

Write a short dialogue (4–6 lines) using at least two idiomatic expressions from this module. Underline the idioms you use.
