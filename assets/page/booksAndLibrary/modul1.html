Modul 1: Principal Strategies in Short Conversations

A. Objectives

After studying this material, students are able to:

  • Put more focus on the last line
  • Find out the similarity in meaning (synonym) between the questions and answer choices
  • Be familiar with the words of similar sounds which may distract or trick them when answering

B. Material Description

1.a. Focus on the Last Line

Pada percakapan pendek, sering kali *clue* atau inti jawaban berada pada kalimat terakhir dari pembicara kedua. Fokuslah ke kalimat ini, karena biasanya mengandung makna tersirat yang menjadi kunci jawaban.

Example 1:

Woman: Do you know where Dean Nicholson’s office is?
Man: There’s a directory in the entrance way.
Narrator: What does the man imply about Dean Nicholson’s office?

Answer: C. He doesn’t know where the office is.

Example 2:

Man: Can I borrow your math notes from yesterday?
Woman: I left them at home.
Narrator: What does the woman imply?
Answer: B. She doesn’t have the notes with her now.

1.b. Answer with Synonyms

Dalam beberapa soal, jawaban tidak menggunakan kata yang sama dengan pertanyaan, tetapi menggunakan synonyms (kata yang maknanya mirip). Kamu harus terbiasa mengenali padanan makna ini agar tidak terkecoh.

Example 1:

Woman: How’s your research project coming, Mike?
Man: So far, so good.
Narrator: What does Mike mean?
Answer: D. Until at the moment, he has not had any problems.

Example 2:

Man: I heard Sarah got a promotion.
Woman: Yes, she’s now the head of marketing.
Narrator: What does the woman say about Sarah?
Answer: B. She received a higher position.

Exercise 1.2

Do the same with the next set of dialogues. Focus on the use of synonyms.

1.c. Avoid Homophones

Homophones adalah kata-kata yang bunyinya mirip atau sama, tapi arti dan penulisannya berbeda. Dalam TOEFL Listening, ini bisa menjebak jika kamu hanya mendengarkan tanpa memperhatikan konteks kalimat.

Example 1:

Man: I’d like coffee with milk, please.
Woman: Would you like a large or a small mug?
Narrator: What did the woman ask?
Answer: D. What size mug the man wants

Example 2:

Woman: Did you hear about the sale?
Man: Yes, I bought a pair.
Narrator: What does the man mean?
Possible Confusion: “pair” (dua) vs “pear” (buah)
Answer: C. He bought two items

Exercise 1.3

Write down the conversations and underline the homophones in the answer choices. Then choose the correct meaning based on the context.

C. Review Exercises

This is a review of the strategies: last line, synonyms, and homophones. Write down the conversation, identify the key elements, and select the best answer.

1. Review Question

Man: I don’t think I can make it to the meeting today.
Woman: That’s okay. I’ll let the manager know.
Narrator: What does the woman mean?
Answer: A. She will inform the manager about the man’s absence.

2. Review Question

Woman: How much is this pair of shoes?
Man: They’re on sale for fifty bucks.
Narrator: What does the man mean?
Answer: B. The shoes cost fifty dollars.

D. Summary of Strategies

Strategy Description What to Watch For
Focus on the Last Line Kalimat terakhir dari pembicara kedua biasanya mengandung kunci jawaban. Implied meaning, suggestions, refusals
Use of Synonyms Jawaban bisa tidak menggunakan kata yang sama persis, tapi maknanya sama. Paraphrasing, alternate vocabulary
Avoid Homophones Kata-kata yang mirip bunyi bisa menjebak jika tidak fokus pada makna konteks. pear/pair, sale/sail, to/too/two
