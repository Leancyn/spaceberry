Module 8: Suggestions & Uncertainty

A. Objectives

After studying this material, students are able to:

  • Understand how suggestions and uncertainty are expressed in English conversation
  • Differentiate between direct suggestions and expressions of uncertainty

B. Material Description

8.a. Suggestions

Suggestions are used to give ideas or advice in a polite and indirect way.

Common phrases include:

  • "Why don’t you...?"
  • "Let’s..."
  • "You could..."
  • "How about...?"
  • "I suggest..."
Man: We should review our script before the presentation.
Woman: That’s a good idea. Let’s do it after lunch.
Answer: A. She agrees and makes a suggestion.

Exercise 8.1

Underline all suggestion expressions in the conversations below and identify who is suggesting what.

8.b. Uncertainty Expressions

Uncertainty is used when someone is unsure or not completely confident in their answer.

Common phrases include:

  • "I’m not sure"
  • "I guess..."
  • "Maybe..."
  • "It seems that..."
  • "Probably..."
Woman: Will the class be cancelled tomorrow?
Man: I guess so. The teacher looked pretty sick today.
Answer: C. He is not sure, but thinks class might be cancelled.

Exercise 8.2

Underline all expressions of uncertainty and explain what the speaker is unsure about.

8.c. Combined Usage

Sometimes, a suggestion and uncertainty appear together.

Man: Should we take the bus or the train?
Woman: Maybe we could take the train. It’s usually faster.
Answer: B. She suggests taking the train but sounds uncertain.

Exercise 8.3

Analyze conversations that include both suggestion and uncertainty. Decide which is being emphasized more.

C. Review Exercises

Determine whether the response in each short dialogue is a suggestion or an expression of uncertainty.

  • A: Clear Suggestion
  • B: Uncertainty
  • C: A mix of both

Sample Review:

Man: Where should we meet them?
Woman: I’m not sure. Maybe at the café near campus?
Answer: C. A mix of uncertainty and suggestion.

D. Summary Table of Key Expressions

Expression Type Meaning
Let’s... Suggestion Inviting someone to do something together
You could... Suggestion Offering an idea politely
Maybe... Uncertainty Not sure, giving a possibility
I’m not sure Uncertainty Clearly indicates doubt
Probably... Uncertainty Something is likely but not certain
How about...? Suggestion Making a casual recommendation
